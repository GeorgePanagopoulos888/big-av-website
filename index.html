Agentic intelligence

Level up with agentic intelligence.

Most automation follows rules. Bradley works toward outcomes.

Agentic intelligence means Bradley can understand what you are trying to accomplish, determine which systems are involved, coordinate the required actions, and verify the result.

That matters because modern environments are no longer just rooms with screens. They are networks of AV systems, lighting, sound, security, conferencing, building controls, cloud software, business applications, and personal workflows.

Bradley connects those systems into one intelligent layer, so your environment can be controlled, expanded, and managed by asking for the outcome, not by hunting through apps, remotes, panels, and tickets.

The interface

The user interface just got smarter.

Bradley lives on the wall panel, on your phone, and in the room by voice. Same scenes, same approvals, same intelligence on every surface.

Touch panels have their place in a well-designed room. We design, program, and support them wherever hardware on the wall makes sense. The difference is where the intelligence lives: above the panel, not locked inside it.

So the interface keeps up with you. Add a button. Swap a display. Adjust a room mode. Connect a new application. Extend to another room. Changes that used to be programming projects become requests, and the room keeps feeling like the same room while the environment evolves.

Traditional control

  • Each room becomes a small programming island
  • Change requests become tickets, delays, and new costs
  • Apps, panels, meetings, lighting, and signage drift apart

Bradley on the layer

  • Panels, voice, scenes, approvals, and apps share one intent layer
  • Room modes and buttons evolve with the space
  • Users ask for outcomes while the system coordinates the details
